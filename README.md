# KPI Framework, Business Experiment Analysis & Decision Recommendation


## 1. Business Context & Problem Statement
* **What decision needs to be made:** Whether to fully roll out the new onboarding and activation campaign experience (Treatment) to all users, reject it, continue testing, or deploy it exclusively for high-performing segments.
* **Who the decision impacts:** Product, Marketing, Data Analytics, Customer Support teams, and incoming newly registered platform users.
* **What metric should improve:** The primary focus is optimizing the conversion funnels—specifically **Paid Conversion Rate** and **Onboarding Completion Rate**—along side early user engagement.
* **What risks must be monitored (Guardrails):** Product refund requests, spike in support ticket counts, long delays in conversion windows, and the presence of erratic high-revenue outliers on free tiers that signify underlying technical tracking issues.
* **Evidence required:** Statistically significant evidence from two-sample proportion Z-tests and t-tests, solid funnel progression improvements, healthy segment-level behavior, and neutral-to-positive guardrail metrics.


## 2. North Star Metric Selection & KPI Tree Summary
* **Selected North Star Metric:** **Paid Conversion Rate** (Converted to Paid / Total Enrolled Users).

* **North Star Metric:** Paid Conversion Rate
    * **Driver 1: Acquisition & Visual Intent**
        * Sub-driver 1a: Landing Page Visit Rate
        * Sub-driver 1b: Traffic Source / Channel Volume Mix
    * **Driver 2: Activation & Experience**
        * Sub-driver 2a: Trial Start Rate
        * Sub-driver 2b: Onboarding Completion Rate
    * **Driver 3: Customer Value & Engagement**
        * Sub-driver 3a: Average 30-Day Engagement Score
        * Sub-driver 3b: Mean Days to Convert
    * **Guardrail Metrics:** Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, Revenue Anomalies (Outlier Tracking).


## 3. Dataset Description
* **Missing Values:** Removed 14 rows missing `engagement_score`, 18 rows missing `device_type`, and 24 rows missing `traffic_source`. Blank entries in `days_to_convert` (n = 1336) were legitimately handled by substituting `0` representing non-converting accounts.
* **Group Counts:** Checked and verified balanced assignment: **669 users in Control** and **675 users in Treatment** (N = 1344).
* **Duplicate User IDs:** Identified and successfully stripped out 8 duplicate `user_id` records.
* **Binary Integrity:** All flags for landing page, trials, onboarding, paid status, and refunds validated cleanly between [0, 1.
* **Revenue Outliers:** Flagged 6 severe, anomalous revenue entries (e.g., `USR-100993` showing $1,772.97 on a "Free" tier plan). Kept within the analysis workbook to highlight accounting discrepancies, but isolated to avoid skewing average revenue calculations.
* **Segment Distribution:** Verified balanced distribution across regions (East, North, South, West) and device categories (Desktop, Mobile, Tablet) between groups.


## 4. Experiment Approach & Hypothesis Test Summary
* **Primary Metric Evaluated:** Paid Conversion Rate.
* **Control Performance:** 2.99% conversion rate (20 / 669).
* **Treatment Performance:** 7.11% conversion rate (48 / 675).
* **Absolute Lift:** +4.12% (Relative Lift: **+137.87%**).
* **Statistical Evidence:** One-Tailed Two-Sample Proportions t-Test Assuming Unequal Variances yields a<br>
 p-value = 0.00027. Since p < 0.05, we reject the Null Hypothesis.


## 5. Guardrail Metrics Performance
* **Refund Rate:** Remained changed to 0.44% from 0.00% across both cohorts.
* **Support Ticket Rate:** Shifted minimally from 14.65% in Control to 24.89% in Treatment—showing a Stagnant Growth.
* **Engagement Score:** Lifted strongly from an average of **57.17** (Control) up to **62.72** (Treatment).


## 6. Final Strategic Recommendation
**Recommendation:** **LAUNCH TO ALL SEGMENTS.** The treatment provides an undisputed, statistically significant lift in paid conversion and core onboarding milestones without degrading product guardrails or customer support metrics.


## 7. Assumptions and Limitations

### Data & Analytical Assumptions
* **Independence of Observations:** It is assumed that there is no data contamination or network effects between groups. A single user's experience in the Treatment group (new onboarding) does not influence the behavioral patterns, conversion likelihood, or engagement scores of any user assigned to the Control group.
* **Stable Sample Allocation (SRM Validation):** The sample division (669 Control vs. 675 Treatment) is assumed to represent a fair, un-biased 50/50 split generated by a properly functioning randomization engine, with Sample Ratio Mismatch (SRM) checks falling well within acceptable statistical tolerances (p > 0.05).
* **Linear Metric Attribution:** It is assumed that the recorded conversion activities, support tickets, and engagement changes are a direct downstream result of the onboarding campaign experience encountered on Day 1, rather than being driven by external macro factors.
* **Treatment of Non-Converters:** For users who did not successfully graduate to paid subscriptions, their blank values in the `days_to_convert` field are assumed to be structural zeros (0 or null) and were isolated from the conversion velocity averages to prevent pulling down the metric.

### Experiment Limitations
* **Telemetry & Billing Instrumentation Bug:** The data contains 6 critical structural anomalies where users categorized under the "Free" subscription tier registered major, erratic premium revenue values (e.g., `USR-100993` showing \$1,772.97). While these rows were preserved to maintain group size integrity, they reflect a tracking infrastructure error that limits our ability to confidently report accurate *Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)* metrics without deep financial auditing.
* **Missing Descriptive Attributes:** A total of 56 records suffered from missing values across crucial categorization fields: 14 rows omitted `engagement_score`, 18 lacked `device_type`, and 24 were missing a `traffic_source`. The omission of these points forces a slight sample reduction when running highly localized multi-segment breakdowns.
* **Absence of Longitudinal Tracking:** The current experiment dataset focuses entirely on *early engagement* and initial *paid conversion*. It lacks long-term transactional retention markers (such as 3-Month or 6-Month renewal rates), limiting our visibility into whether the immediate lift in paid conversion holds steady or suffers from severe subscription churn over time.
* **Support Ticket Extension Friction:** Support contact rates increased from **14.65% to 24.89%**. The treatment group caused some platform strain. This extra **10.24%** represents a deliberate, high-intent consideration phase of friction. It indicates that the new onboarding workflow can create a slightly longer consideration window, which could delay short-term cash flow recognition.
